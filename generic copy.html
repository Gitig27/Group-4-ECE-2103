Improving Dredging?

To enhance dredging efforts in the Pansipit River, real-time monitoring systems should be introduced to track water quality and sediment levels, enabling quick responses to pollution spikes. Additionally, implementing wastewater treatment facilities would prevent untreated sewage from entering the river, addressing the root cause of pollution and reducing the need for frequent dredging. The municipality could also promote sustainable agricultural and industrial practices to minimize runoff pollutants, offering incentives to businesses and farmers. An advocacy website can play a crucial role by sharing real-time data, educational resources, and tips for reducing pollution. It can feature an interactive map showing dredging activities, provide a reporting tool for pollution, and organize community clean-up efforts. This website would engage locals in protecting the river and encourage collective action for a cleaner, healthier Pansipit River.
